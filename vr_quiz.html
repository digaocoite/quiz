Virtual Reality Quiz

1. What are the four key elements of Virtual Reality?

Graphics, Interaction, Feedback, Sound
Virtual World, Immersion, Sensory Feedback, Interactivity
AI, 3D Modeling, Textures, Physics

Explanation: VR consists of Virtual World, Immersion, Sensory Feedback, and Interactivity.

2. What does 'immersion' in VR refer to?

A video game with 3D graphics
A sense of presence in an alternate reality
The ability to move an avatar in a 2D game

Explanation: Immersion is about feeling present in a virtual space.

3. What is an example of Augmented Reality?

Playing a VR horror game
Watching a 3D movie
Using AR glasses to overlay information on the real world

Explanation: AR overlays digital content onto the real world, unlike VR which creates a fully virtual environment.

4. What device is commonly used for VR immersion?

Smartphone
Television
Head-Mounted Display (HMD)

Explanation: HMDs like Oculus or Vive allow users to experience VR.

5. What is haptic feedback in VR?

A type of visual enhancement
A network protocol for VR connections
A technology that provides touch sensations

Explanation: Haptic feedback lets users "feel" virtual objects through vibrations or force.

6. Which industry is NOT a major adopter of VR?

Healthcare
Gaming
Agriculture

Explanation: While agriculture uses technology, VR is more prominent in healthcare and gaming.

7. What does "field of view" mean in VR?

The number of objects in a VR scene
The extent of the observable virtual environment
The range of a VR device's sensors

Explanation: Field of View (FOV) determines how much of the VR world you can see at once.

8. What is motion sickness in VR caused by?

High-resolution graphics
A mismatch between visual input and inner ear balance
Slow internet connection

Explanation: Motion sickness happens when the brain receives conflicting signals from the eyes and inner ear.

9. What is a VR avatar?

A robot inside a VR world
A game developer's tool
A virtual representation of a user in VR

Explanation: Avatars represent users in a VR environment.

10. What is a "CAVE" system in VR?

A VR game level
A VR storage device
A room-sized immersive VR system

Explanation: A CAVE (Cave Automatic Virtual Environment) uses projections to create an immersive VR experience.
